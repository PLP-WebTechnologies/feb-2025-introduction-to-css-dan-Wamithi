Masai Mara National Reserve

The Masai Mara National Reserve is one of Africa's most magnificent game reserves. The preserve is named after the Maasai people who inhabit the area and the Mara River that flows through it.

Famous for its exceptional population of lions, leopards, and cheetahs, and the annual migration of zebra, Thomson's gazelle, and wildebeest from the Serengeti plains to the Masai Mara, which happens every year from July to October.

The reserve also hosts over 450 bird species and is characterized by rolling grasslands, acacia woodlands, and riverine forests divided by the Mara River.

Best Time to Visit

July to October for the Great Migration, but wildlife viewing is excellent year-round.

Amboseli National Park

Amboseli National Park offers the classic image of African safari: herds of elephants against the backdrop of Africa's highest mountain, Mt. Kilimanjaro, which rises just across the border in Tanzania.

The park is famous for being the best place in Africa to get close to free-ranging elephants. Other attractions include opportunities to meet the Maasai people and spectacular views of Mount Kilimanjaro when the weather is clear.

The park also features diverse wildlife including giraffes, zebras, cheetahs, and hundreds of bird species, alongside five different habitats ranging from the dried-up bed of Lake Amboseli to wetlands with sulphur springs.

Best Time to Visit

June to October and January to February for clear views of Kilimanjaro and dry conditions ideal for game viewing.

Diani Beach

Diani Beach is one of Kenya's most popular coastal destinations, known for its powder-white sand, lush greenery, and the warm waters of the Indian Ocean.

The beach stretches for about 10 kilometers along the coast and offers excellent conditions for swimming, kitesurfing, diving, and snorkeling. The offshore coral reef provides protection from sharks and strong tides, making it a safe spot for swimming.

Beyond beach activities, visitors can explore the Kaya Kinondo Sacred Forest, go on a dhow cruise, or visit the Colobus Conservation center which works to protect the area's rare Angolan colobus monkeys.

Best Time to Visit

January to March and July to October for warm, dry weather with less humidity.

Kenya Travel Tips

Essential advice for planning your perfect Kenya adventure.

Best Time to Visit

Kenya has two dry seasons: January to March and July to October. These periods offer the best wildlife viewing with less vegetation and animals gathering around water sources.

The Great Migration reaches the Masai Mara from July to October, but accommodations book quickly during this period.

Visa Information

Most visitors require a visa to enter Kenya. E-visas can be obtained online before travel through the official government portal.

Single-entry tourist visas are valid for 90 days and can be extended once you're in the country if needed.

Health & Safety

Yellow fever vaccination is required for entry. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for most regions.

Travel insurance is essential. Drink only bottled or purified water and use insect repellent, especially in the evenings.

Safari Packing List

Pack light, neutral-colored clothing (avoid blue, black and bright colors), a wide-brimmed hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, and binoculars.

Mornings and evenings can be cool, so bring layers even during summer months.
